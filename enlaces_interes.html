Founding the Legacy: The Origins of Sailing Pro

In the serene coastal town of Fairhope, Alabama, nestled along the picturesque shores of Mobile Bay, a passion for sailing and a commitment to excellence converged to lay the foundation for what would become Sailing Pro. It all began in the early 1990s when Jack Thompson, a former national sailing champion, returned to his hometown of Fairhope after years of competitive sailing around the world. Inspired by his own journey in the sport and driven by a desire to share his expertise, Jack envisioned creating a place where aspiring sailors could receive top-tier coaching in a nurturing environment. With the support of local sailing enthusiasts and the guidance of his mentor, a seasoned sailing coach from New Zealand, Jack established Sailing Pro in 1995. The initial setup was modestâ€”a small boathouse by the Fairhope Yacht Club, equipped with a handful of boats and a dedicated team of instructors.


From the outset, Sailing Pro differentiated itself by focusing not only on technical skill but also on fostering a love for the sea and a deep respect for the art of sailing. Jack's vision attracted talented coaches from diverse sailing backgrounds, each bringing their unique experiences and coaching philosophies to enrich the program. Over the years, Sailing Pro grew in reputation, drawing students from across the Gulf Coast and beyond. Its commitment to excellence and personalized coaching tailored to each sailor's goals earned it recognition as a premier sailing academy in the region.
